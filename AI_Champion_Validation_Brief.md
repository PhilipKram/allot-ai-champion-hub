## 1. Program Overview

The AI Champion Enablement Training equips newly selected champions across R&D, Product, and Business teams with the skills and confidence to drive distributed AI adoption at Allot. Champions serve as the bridge between centralized AI strategy and local team implementation — acting as evangelists, first responders, quality gates, and trainers. The program moves beyond basic tool usage into production-grade, secure AI integration.

## 3. Key Design Decisions

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **V-C-A-F Framework** (Verify, Contextualize, Authorize, Fairness Check) | Central verification habit taught from pre-work through Day 2. Ensures champions don't just use AI — they verify facts, check business context, take accountability, and monitor for bias. |
| **Track A/B Split** (Technical vs. Business) | R&D champions need code review and log analysis workflows; Product/Business champions need sentiment analysis and user story generation. One-size-fits-all doesn't work. |
| **Mandatory 5–6 hour Pre-Work** | Moves fundamentals out of the workshop so both days focus on hands-on practice, not lectures. Includes security exercises so attendees arrive with the right habits. |
| **Resistance Roleplay Exercises** | The champion role is about influence, not just skills. Three realistic scenarios (skeptical dev, anxious junior, overwhelmed manager) build the soft skills champions actually need. |
| **Incident Response Drill** | A live-fire simulation where champions practice handling an accidental data leak — because reading a policy isn't the same as following it under pressure. |
| **Feasibility vs. Impact Matrix** | Gives champions a systematic framework for identifying where AI adds value, not just where it's interesting. |

## 5. Questions for the Validation Group

We need your input on the following before proceeding:

1. **V-C-A-F Framework** — Does this meet our requirements for responsible AI usage? Should the Fairness Check be mandatory for all outputs, or only for people-related analysis as currently designed?

2. **Track A/B Split** — Is the Technical / Business split sufficient, or do specific teams (e.g., Support, Legal, HR) need a dedicated third track?

3. **Success Metrics** — Are the proposed champion KPIs (60%+ team adoption in 3 months, 10+ queries/week per active user, workshops delivered, use cases documented) aligned with organizational goals?

4. **Pre-Work Commitment** — Is 5–6 hours of mandatory preparation acceptable given champion workloads? Should we reduce scope or extend the deadline?

5. **Capstone Realism** — The capstone asks teams to build a working AI workflow in 45 minutes. Is this the right level of ambition, or should we adjust expectations?

6. **Missing Topics** — Is there anything critical to Allot's AI strategy that isn't covered in the current curriculum?
