Your pre-work — 2.5 hours, before Day 1

Start now · tool access takes 2–3 days

The workshop is hands-on and fast-paced. This pre-work gets everyone to a shared baseline so we can skip the basics and go straight to practice. Budget ~2.5 hours, spread across this week. Progress saves locally in your browser.

1
Tool setup & access
30 min · do this FIRST · license requests may take 2–3 business days
Verify you can log in to Claude. If you don't have access, email Philip Kramer (pkramer@allot.com) with subject "AI Tools License Request" — not IT Service Desk. Bookmark the AI Champions - Training Teams channel and the Allot AI usage policy.
2
AI foundations — core concepts
45 min · read, don't memorize
LLM behavior (generates plausible, not true), key terminology, and — most importantly — the Data Classification table. You'll teach that table to your team; you need to know it cold.
3
First steps with AI — two exercises
30 min · outputs come to Day 1
(3.1) Draft a prompt using the Inigo 4-part frame — we'll rewrite it together in Session 3. (3.2) Ask Claude a factual question in your domain, then apply V-C-A-F to its answer and note what it got wrong.
4
Security sandbox — sanitization challenge
15 min
The "Firehawk transcript" exercise: rewrite a raw meeting note so it's safe to send to an AI tool. Bring your sanitized version on Day 1 — we'll debrief common mistakes.
5
Domain deep-dive — pick one
30 min · R&D, Product/Business, or Security
R&D: write a unit test with AI, apply V-C-A-F. Product/Business: draft a user story from a feature brief, apply V-C-A-F. Security: explain a recent CVE in plain language, apply V-C-A-F. Bring the output and your notes.

What to bring — and what to leave out

Bring on Day 1

  • Your laptop with approved AI tools already installed & tested (do NOT install on the day of).
  • 2–3 real work examples you'd like to try with AI — sanitized per the Data Classification table.
  • Your pre-work outputs: Inigo-frame prompt, V-C-A-F notes, sanitized Firehawk transcript, domain deep-dive artifact.
  • A problem you'd love to solve — something your team hits repeatedly. This becomes your capstone.
  • Headset if you're joining remotely — breakout rooms need clear audio.

Do not bring / do not paste

  • Credentials, API keys, tokens — ever. Not in a prompt, not in a code snippet.
  • Customer PII or raw customer data — sanitize or synthesize first.
  • NDA-protected specs, internal architecture details marked Restricted.
  • Unsanitized log files or config files — strip secrets first.
  • A "just popping out" mindset — the format is pair work & labs; leaving breaks your partner's day.

FAQ

Is the pre-work graded? What if I don't finish it?
No gating exam on pre-work — but if you skip it, Day 1 will be significantly harder because sessions build on what you've already tried. The only graded piece is the Certification Exam, which comes after the 2 days.
I already use Claude every day. Do I still need to do the pre-work?
Yes — the pre-work outputs (Inigo prompt, V-C-A-F notes, sanitized Firehawk transcript) are inputs to specific Day 1 exercises. You'll work on them, not re-do them.
I can't get Claude access in time — what do I do?
Email Philip Kramer (pkramer@allot.com) with subject "AI Tools License Request" this week. Do NOT route through IT Service Desk — that slows it down. License provisioning takes 2–3 business days.
Can I drop in for part of a day?
No. The format is pair work and labs that build on each other. Leaving mid-session breaks your partner's experience as well as your own. Block the full two days in your calendar — we've briefed your line manager.
What if my real work example contains sensitive data?
Sanitize it. Replace customer names, IDs, credentials, and specifics with placeholders or synthetic equivalents. Pre-work Part 4 (the Firehawk exercise) is the drill for this — finish it before Day 1 and you'll know what "sanitized enough" looks like.
Will the sessions be recorded?
Recording policy will be confirmed in your calendar invite closer to the date. Lab and capstone portions won't be recorded either way — too much live work, too much sensitive discussion.
What happens if I miss the certification deadline (11 May)?
Reach out directly — a short extension can usually be arranged if you flag it early. Silently missing the deadline is the problem, not the delay itself.
